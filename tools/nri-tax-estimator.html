How NRI Taxation Works in FY 2025-26

As a Non-Resident Indian, you are only liable to pay income tax in India on income that is earned or accrued in India. Your foreign salary, overseas bank interest, and international investments are not taxable in India and should not appear in your Indian Income Tax Return.

What is taxable are five main categories: NRO account interest, rental income from Indian property, capital gains from Indian assets (shares, mutual funds, property), dividends from Indian companies, and any salary received in India for work done in India.

The New Tax Regime is Now Default

Budget 2025 made the new tax regime the default for all taxpayers. If you do not actively opt for the old regime, you will be assessed under the new slabs. For most NRIs who have limited India-specific deductions, the new regime often results in lower tax — but always run both calculations if you have Indian property loan interest or significant 80C investments.

NRIs Do Not Get Section 87A Rebate

This is one of the most common misunderstandings. The Section 87A rebate makes income up to ₹7 lakh effectively tax-free for resident Indians. NRIs are explicitly excluded from this rebate. NRIs start paying tax from ₹3 lakh upward (under the new regime's basic exemption) with no rebate to offset the liability.

Capital Gains Rates After July 2024

Budget 2024 (effective 23 July 2024) introduced significant capital gains changes that carry into FY 2025-26. Equity LTCG moved to 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh per year (up from 10% above ₹1 lakh). Equity STCG rose to 20% (from 15%). Property LTCG is now 12.5% without indexation — the old 20% with indexation no longer applies to sales after 23 July 2024.

TDS Is Deducted Automatically — Filing Allows Refunds

Banks deduct TDS on NRO interest at 30% automatically. Brokers and AMCs deduct TDS on equity gains at redemption. Buyers of your property deduct TDS from the sale proceeds. All of this TDS is visible in your Form 26AS. When you file your ITR, if the TDS deducted exceeds your actual tax liability — which is common — the difference is refunded to your pre-validated bank account.

Using DTAA to Reduce Tax

If India has a Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement with your country of residence (the UAE, US, UK, Canada, and Singapore are all covered), you may be able to claim reduced rates on certain income types. The India-UAE DTAA, for example, caps TDS on NRO interest at 12.5% — lower than the standard 30%. To claim this, you need a Tax Residency Certificate from the UAE Federal Tax Authority and must file Form 10F and complete Schedules FSI and TR in your ITR-2.
